On my year abroad in Melbourne I pursued additional rocketry experience alongside my full-time research. My goal was to gain insight into the workings of the world-class rocketry team, ARES, and understand their engineering workflow. I was privileged to join the team as a temporary Structures engineer, and work on the IREC-25 competition rocket, <a href="https://eng.unimelb.edu.au/ares/projects#lemaire">Lemaire</a>.

I contributed to the design of the structural housing for the onbaord flight computer, specifically introducing ease of access for maintenance, and modularity of FC PCBs. I also participated in a suite of workshop training programmes offered by the Telstra Creator Space (partnered with the University of Melbourne), attended the preliminary design review, and attended a launch day hosted by Victoria Rocketry Association. 

While my contribution to the team was limited, the insight and experience I gained from my stint in ARES was invaluable - showing me the design philosophy and collaboration expected in a high-performance engineering team.
