In highschool I took graphic communication classes in my final two years. I’m including my experience from these classes to provide some pre-text for my engineering projects – after all, this experience was a very good foundation.

This course introduced me to the concept of CAD, the basic theory, and how to make products for use in advertising. The primary skills I continue to use from this experience are:
-	3D-CAD: how to use Autodesk Inventor, sketching and 3D modelling tools, how to create parts from technical drawings, how to create parts of reasonable complexity, and how to assemble these parts to make a product.
-	Technical Drawings: how to adhere to BSI and 3rd-angle projection, how to dimension effectively, and how to communicate complex features with section and magnified views.  

These are the skills that later got me into endeavour as a Structures Engineer. An example of an assignment I completed is below: designing earphones and a case. As shown, I made three parts of the earphone, and assembled and rendered the product. Next, I made technical drawings for the parts individually, and for the full assembly. Finally, I sketched the case.
