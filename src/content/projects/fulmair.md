My first engineering project was my work on endeavour's rocket for the Spaceport America 2024 competition, Fulmair - specifically on the upper airframe. This rocket aimed to carry a 4 kilogram payload to 3 km, on an O-class motor. I was tasked with the design of the nose cone, payload & recovery bays, and payload & recovery bulkheads.

I collaborated with the Payload and Recovery teams to iteratively inform the design of their respective components, ensuring ease of use (accessibility, simple design, functional geometry). Equipped with prior CAD and technical drawing experience, I took the initiative to rapidly prototype these parts for design reviews, easing workload from my teammates and exceeding design timelines. 

![Recovery bulkhead technical drawing](/projects/engineering/fulmair-rcbk-dwg.png)
![Nose cone FEA study](/projects/engineering/fulmair-nc-fea.png)

After some slight tweaking by team leads, these components were fully manufactured (aside from the nose cone), in preparation for the competition. Unfortunately, abrupt cuts to university funding prevented the team from attending the competition, however the rocket remains ready to fly once a nose cone is manufactured (as well as critical internal components).

This project taught me a lot about collaborative engineering and iterative design, and was also a lot of fun to work on. I didn't get much exposure to manufacturing, but this was nonetheless very valuable experience.
